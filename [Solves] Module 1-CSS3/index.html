Weekly Community Bulletin

Council Approves New Green Space Initiatives

The city council voted unanimously this past Tuesday afternoon to convert the historical, abandoned rail yard on Elm Street into an expansive public park and community garden project. Construction is slated to officially begin late next month, with pathways, native flora plantings, and recreational zones expected to open by early winter. Residents are encouraged to submit structural ideas for the playground section online.

In addition to the park space, structural grants have been allocated toward planting urban canopy trees along high-traffic pedestrian corridors, looking to combat summer heat index spikes effectively.
